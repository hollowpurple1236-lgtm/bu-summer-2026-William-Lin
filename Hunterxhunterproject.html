Series Overview

A quick orientation before diving into the archive — what the story follows, and the four pillars this reference is organized around.

01

The Premise

A young boy sets out to become a licensed Hunter, an elite profession granting access to rare resources, restricted areas, and dangerous jobs — chiefly to track down the father who abandoned him.

02

Hunters

Hunters aren't a single job but a credential. Licensed individuals branch into specialties: Blacklist Hunters chase criminals, Treasure Hunters hunt artifacts, Beast Hunters study wildlife, and more.

03

Nen

Power in this world runs on Nen, a system for channeling life energy into offense, defense, or transformation. It has firm categories and personal rules that make every user's ability distinct.

04

Tone

The series is known for shifting registers — breezy adventure one arc, psychological horror or political intrigue the next — while keeping its cast's internal logic consistent throughout.

The Nen System

Nen is the art of channeling one's own life energy (aura) for combat and utility. Practitioners fall into one of six broad categories based on natural aptitude, though most learn to use several.

Core Principles

Ten

Containment

Holding aura inside the body instead of leaking it out, the foundation every other technique builds on.

Zetsu

Suppression

Shutting off aura flow entirely — useful for hiding presence, at the cost of leaving the body undefended.

Ren

Amplification

Deliberately releasing more aura than natural leakage, raising output for a stronger effect.

Ryu

Rotation

Concentrating aura in a single body part to sharpen a specific technique.

Ken

Defense

Wrapping aura around the whole body as an armor-like guard against outside attacks.

Gyo

Focus

Concentrating aura in the eyes to see other people's aura and read hidden techniques.

The Hunter Exam

An annual, invitation-by-rumor test that only a fraction of applicants survive. Its structure changes every year, but it always narrows a huge starting field down to a handful of new Hunters.

01

The Gathering

Hopefuls first have to locate the exam itself — the time and place are deliberately obscure, filtering out anyone who can't find the starting line.

02

Physical Trials

Early phases typically test raw stamina and navigation — long treks, races against time, or survival in hostile terrain.

03

Skill & Judgment

Middle phases lean on specialized skills — cooking, tracking, puzzle-solving — chosen by that year's examiners to reward preparation and adaptability, not just strength.

04

Direct Combat

Later phases pit applicants directly against one another, forcing decisions about when to fight and when a win isn't worth the cost.

05

Final Judgment

A final interview or vote from the examiners decides who receives the license, often weighing character as heavily as capability.
